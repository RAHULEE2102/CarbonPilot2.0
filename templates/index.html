The Problem, The Product, The Proof

01 · What it is
Fleet CO₂ Intelligence.
No Fuel Sensor. Protocol-agnostic.

CarbonPilot is an AI inference platform that estimates real-time CO₂ emissions from any vehicle — passenger car or commercial truck — using only standard OBD-II and SAE J1939 telematics signals already present on every modern vehicle.

It runs the LBCA-Net deep learning model (1.24M parameters, 4.9 MB, 3.1 ms inference) and produces ISO 14064-aligned Scope 1 emission reports at 1-Hz resolution — without any additional hardware, fuel-flow sensor, or cloud dependency for inference. Note: training-set ground-truth labels were derived from OBD fuel-channel data in the Brazil fleet dataset; fuel-flow PID is not required at inference time.

02 · Why it exists
Three market failures no one has solved.
$200–500/vehicle deployment tax
Many light-duty vehicles do not expose fuel-flow PID (OBD 0x5E) — the ECU either does not implement it or the adapter filters it — affecting a significant share of real-world deployments. CarbonPilot does not require this PID at inference.
Protocol fragmentation costs double
Zero published models cross OBD-II (cars) ↔ SAE J1939 (trucks). Mixed fleets need two vendors, two calibrations, two pipelines. CarbonPilot bridges both in one model.
Idle-regime bias in CSRD Scope 1
All prior models predict g/km only — undefined at zero speed. Urban trucks idle 35.2% of engine-on time. CarbonPilot's dual-regime head covers both g/km and g/min in one forward pass.

The Market Gap

Three Barriers No Product Has Solved — Until Now

⛽
Fuel Sensor Lock-In
Many existing CO₂ models require fuel-flow PID (0x5E) as input. This PID is frequently absent because the vehicle ECU does not expose it — creating a significant deployment barrier. CarbonPilot is trained and deployed without this PID at inferencers, creating a $200–$500/vehicle deployment barrier that eliminates most real-world fleet deployments before they start.
⚡
Protocol Fragmentation
Zero published models cross the OBD-II (SAE J1979, passenger cars) to SAE J1939 (commercial trucks) protocol boundary. Mixed fleets — the industry norm — require two entirely separate vendor contracts, two calibration cycles, two reporting pipelines.
🅿️
Idle Period Blindspot
All prior models predict CO₂ in g/km — a unit undefined at zero speed. Urban truck (Truck-3) operates at idle 35.2% of engine-on time. Ignoring this introduces an 18.3% negative bias in trip-total CO₂, making CSRD Scope 1 reporting systematically non-compliant.

System Architecture

LBCA-Net: Leakage-Free Behavior-Aware Cross-class Adaptive Network

A five-component deep learning architecture operating end-to-end from raw CAN bus signals to CSRD-compliant emission reports at 1-Hz resolution with 3.1ms inference latency on commodity CPU hardware.

Working Principle

From Raw CAN Bus to CSRD Report in 5 Steps

01
Signal Ingestion
Dual-Protocol CAN Bus Acquisition
Raw signals are polled at 1 Hz from either SAE J1979 (OBD-II, passenger cars via ELM327 adapter) or SAE J1939 (commercial trucks via CAN gateway). Six shared signals — engine speed, load, vehicle speed, coolant temperature, derived acceleration, and throttle — form the cross-protocol backbone. J1939 vehicles additionally expose torque, gear position, IMU axes, brake and clutch switches, providing 14 total features. Fuel-flow PID is never requested, removing the primary deployment barrier.
SAE J1979SAE J1939 1-Hz streamingELM327 compatible
02
Domain Alignment
Multi-Kernel signal alignment Latent Space Projection
Protocol-specific linear encoders project each domain's signals into a shared unified signal space representation. A cross-protocol calibration layer minimises the signal distribution difference between car and truck sensor streams. The empirical alignment gap reduces from 0.567 to 0.184 (−67.5%) after alignment, enabling zero-shot car-to-truck generalisation without any labelled truck training data. Activated in the second phase of model training — car-to-truck generalisation is trained without truck ground-truth data.
alignment layerunified signal space signal layer Zero-shot transferλ_DA = 0.10
03
Temporal Modeling
Behavior-Augmented 3-Layer LSTM Encoding
Five window-level behavior descriptors computed over 64-second sliding windows are concatenated with the protocol-aligned representation before entering a 3-layer stacked LSTM [128→256→128] with layer normalisation and dropout (p = 0.2). The behavior descriptors — acceleration variance driving metric, stop-go frequency, idle ratio, high-load fraction, and gear-utilisation entropy (J1939 only) — capture driving-style variance that instantaneous signals cannot encode. The LSTM produces a 128-dimensional hidden state h^LSTM capturing the full temporal context of the current window. Inference cost: O(W) vs. O(W²) for attention, critical for 1-Hz on-vehicle deployment.
W=64s window50% overlap LayerNorm3.1ms CPU inference
04
Regime-Aware Prediction
Gated Dual-Output Head with Willans Constraint
A sigmoid regime classifier ẑₜ ∈ [0,1] gates between two physically distinct output modes: a motion head predicting CO₂ in g km⁻¹ (valid when v > 5 km h⁻¹) and an idle head predicting g min⁻¹ (valid at standstill). The final prediction ŷₜ = ẑₜ·ŷ^(m) + (1−ẑₜ)·ŷ^(i) covers the full operating envelope. A soft Willans-line physics penalty L_phy = mean(max(0, ŷₜ − (α̂·Lₜωₜ + β̂₀))²) with λ_phy = 0.05 bounds predictions to thermodynamically feasible regions, suppressing LSTM over-predictions at low load and high speed without constraining valid predictions below the line.
Dual-regimeWillans physics Softplus activationF1=0.92–0.94
05
Fleet Reporting
Trip-Level CO₂ Accounting and CSRD Export
Per-second predictions are accumulated into trip-level CO₂ totals: E_trip = Σₜ [ẑₜ·(ŷ^(m)ₜ·vₜ/3600) + (1−ẑₜ)·(ŷ^(i)ₜ/60)] kg. Motion and idle contributions are reported separately, enabling precise identification of idle reduction opportunities. For high-idle-fraction truck operation (Truck-3: 35.2% idle), this separation reduces trip-total CO₂ bias from 18.3% (motion-only model) to 3.7%. Output feeds directly into EU CSRD 2022/2464 Scope 1 disclosures, ISO 14064-1 GHG inventories, and usage-based fleet insurance telematics at vehicle-day granularity.
